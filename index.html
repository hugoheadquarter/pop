Built on real memory science

The forgetting curve is real. So is the fix.

In 1885, Hermann Ebbinghaus measured how fast memories fade — within days, most of what you learn is gone.

The fix is reviewing at the right moment, just before you forget. Poppy times every review for you, so a few minutes a day beats hours of cramming.

Questions, answered

Is Poppy free?

You can try Poppy for free. Keeping full access afterwards takes a subscription — one plan, everything included, cancel anytime.

I can't read Korean or Japanese yet. Can I still start?

Yes — that's exactly who Poppy is for. Complete beginners start with the alphabet itself, one letter balloon at a time, and graduate into words.

How is this different from flashcards?

Flashcards test you. Poppy plays with you — falling balloons, hearts, combos, golden rounds — while a serious spaced-repetition engine quietly schedules everything underneath.

Which devices does it work on?

iPhone and iPad, with one subscription covering both. Your learning progress lives on each device.

How much time do I need?

A Learn session is about three and a half minutes for ten words. A few minutes a day is genuinely enough — the schedule does the heavy lifting.
